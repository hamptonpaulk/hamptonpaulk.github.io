Simple Pleasures

Written by Hampton Paulk on Tue 07 July 2015

Here is one of his poems titled The Peace of Wild Things.

I am so grateful to have read even a little Wendell Berry's work. Here is one of his poems titled The Peace of Wild Things.

When despair grows in me and I wake in the night at the least sound in fear of what my life and my children's lives may be,
I go and lie down where the wood drake rests in his beauty on the water, and the great heron feeds. I come into the peace of wild things who do not tax their lives with forethought of grief. I come into the presence of still water. And I feel above me the day-blind stars waiting for their light. For a time I rest in the grace of the world, and am free.

Source: Collected Poems 1957-1982 (Counterpoint Press, 1985)
