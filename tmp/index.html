Video: Claude in Conversation

(6 minutes)

This video demonstrates how to have better conversations with Claude by making small but powerful changes to your prompts. We start with the idea that, like any good conversation, talking to Claude works best when you know what you want to say and why. You’ll see how being specific, giving context, or including examples, helps Claude give more relevant and thoughtful answers. We also introduces extended thinking, a feature that allows Claude to take extra time to reflect, especially when working with complex ideas.

Key takeaways

  • Effective prompting starts with the same habits as good conversation: clarity, intent, and consideration for your audience.
  • Four practical techniques for better results with Claude:
    • Be explicit: Clearly state what you're asking for, including names, dates, or source materials.
    • Give context: Share your purpose or situation so Claude can align its response to your goals.
    • Provide examples: Show the format or structure you want, or give sample inputs and outputs.
    • Enable extended thinking: Use Claude’s built-in tool to let it take time for deeper reasoning.
  • Better prompts create more meaningful responses. Adding structure or background helps Claude return clearer and more useful results.
  • Prompting is a conversation. Building on prior turns, refining your ask, and collaborating with the model leads to better outcomes over time.

Exercises

Hands-on

Try these ideas on your own

Be Explicit & Give Context

What author should I read first on the topic of media studies?

Ask Claude

Then Try:

I’m new to media studies and looking for a readable, foundational book that introduces major concepts. I prefer engaging writing over dense theory. What author and book would you recommend as a starting point?

Ask Claude

 

With & Without Extended Thinking

Could you briefly explain two or three major concepts from those authors?

Ask Claude

Then Try:

I’d like a deeper understanding of two or three major concepts from those authors. Could you take some time to think through their work in more depth? Please identify the key ideas they propose, and show the most relevant counter to their ideas.

Ask Claude

Reflection

Before moving on, think about these ideas:

  • How could you restart some of your older conversations with more detail?
  • What about your approach might change when conversing with an AI like Claude?
  • How might you affect Claude's responses, especially for formatting or tone?
  • What kinds of tasks in learning might require deeper AI reasoning?.

Take a few moments to explore each your answers for these in a conversation with Claude
